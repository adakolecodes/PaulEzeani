Project Overview

The Project
Artstory is an international art gallery with galleries across Africa, Europe, Asia, and South America. Artstory focuses on students and working-class art enthusiasts living in rural and urban areas. Artstory showcases and sells great artwork done by legendary artists the world over. Artstory is a place where art is not just displayed but also explained

Project Duration
August 2022 to December 2022

The Problem
To help art lovers, educators, and advocates who buy tickets or paintings

The Goal
Design an app for Artstory that lets art lovers, educators, and advocates explore the richness of art

My Role
UX designer, designing an app for Artstory's gallery from conception to completion

Responsibilities
Performing user interviews, paper-based and digital wireframing, low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototyping, usability testing, taking accessibility into account, and modifying designs

User research: summary

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I'm designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was art educators and advocates who want to teach people the importance of art.
This user group confirmed the primary hypotheses about Artstory customers, but research also revealed that the lack of a well-structured ticket booking system was not the only factor restricting users from going to the gallery. Other user problems included translation, distance, and finance, which make it problematic to go to the gallery or book tickets. 

Meet the users: Tutti & Caroline

Problem statement: Tutti is an art enthusiast who needs an art gallery that he can visit occasionally because he needs to buy some paintings for his new home.

Problem statement: Caroline is an art educator and advocate who needs a gallery where she can take her students on a tour because she believes art can be a force for good in our society.

Takeaways

Impact

Users of the app are given the impression that Artstory genuinely cares about those who appreciate art, teach it, and promote it.
One comment from a peer said,
"This is an app for voiceless artists." 

What I learned

I discovered that the process doesn't stop with the initial app concepts while creating the Artsyory app. Each iteration of the app's designs was impacted by usability tests and user input
